Welcome to NA Percussion!


Please check back often for updated performance media and educational resources for all things percussion.

This website is currently a proof of concept while I continue to build out projects and ideas as a developer. It is not a finished product yet!

Programming and web development interest me because I'm not just interested in the "whys" of the world -- I'm interested in the "hows."
I've been casually coding on and off since I was 10 years old, but decided to pursue it full-force beginning in 2019. I'm continuing to
build my skill set in both front- and back-end languages, and love discovering new ways in which I can implement my code.
I've begun to fall in love with coding because it resonates with me as a musician. I've discovered so many parallels between the two discliplines:

  • The learning never stops. Every day is a loop of applying what I know, discovering something new, and building it into my vocabulary.
  • The variety of languages and applications for them are endless, much like the variety of musical genres in the world. I can share the stage with
    two of the exact same sets of musicians but play extremely different music in a symphony orchestra versus a jazz band, for example. In that same
    vein, two people with the job title "programmer" are almost certainly following entirely different career paths, using entirely different languages.
  • The programming community that I've discovered thus far into my journey has been supportive and endlessly helpful. Coding is about collaboration and
    creating something greater than any of us could do as individuals. Music works exactly the same way. We collaborate so that we grow beyond ourselves!
  • Programming and music both make use of the left and right brain. They both involve a constant mental loop of figuring out how best to implement an idea
    efficiently or cleanly, and appealing to the senses while doing so.
Thank you for your interest in my website. Please check back often as I continue to update it with multimedia and coding projects of mine.
